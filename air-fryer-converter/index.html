The 20/25 rule

I genuinely don't know why air fryer manuals make this sound so complicated. Most people stick to the "20/25 rule" and it works perfectly: reduce the temperature by 25°F (or 15°C) and reduce the time by 20% to 25%.

Because an air fryer is smaller and the fan is much more powerful than a traditional oven, the hot air hits the food faster and more directly. If you try to cook a chicken breast at 400°F for 20 minutes like you would in a big oven, you’ll end up with a dry, blackened rock. Use this calculator, and most importantly: always check the food a few minutes early. Every air fryer model is a little different.
