Planning Your Handmade Wardrobe

I genuinely believe that knitting is the ultimate slow fashion, but "slow" shouldn't mean "surprise deadlines." Whether you're knitting a gift or a sweater for yourself, knowing your pace helps you manage expectations and avoid the dreaded "WIP" (Work In Progress) pileup.

Calculating Stitches: To get an accurate count, look at your pattern. Multiply the average number of stitches per row by the total number of rows. Don't forget to account for ribbing and sleeves!

Knitting Efficiency Tips:

  • Swatch First: Knitting a gauge swatch not only ensures your garment fits but also gives you a perfect 10-minute window to measure your actual stitches-per-minute speed.
  • Ergonomics: Take breaks every 45 minutes to stretch your hands and neck. A comfortable knitter is a fast knitter.
  • Project Bag: Keep your pattern, tape measure, and extra yarn in one place to reduce "setup time" when you have 15 minutes to spare.
