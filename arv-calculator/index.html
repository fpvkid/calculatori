What is the 70% Rule?

I genuinely believe that the 70% rule is the only thing keeping house flippers from going bankrupt. It states that an investor should pay no more than 70% of the After Repair Value (ARV) of a property, minus the cost of the repairs needed.

Why 70%? The remaining 30% isn't just "pure profit." It has to cover your closing costs, holding costs (taxes, utilities, insurance), financing interest, and the selling commission. If you buy at 85% of ARV, you aren't an investor—вы просто покупаете работу (you're just buying a job).

Common ARV Mistakes:

  • Bad Comps: Comparing a 3-bed house to a 4-bed house across town.
  • Underestimating Repairs: Forgetting about permits, foundation issues, or hidden mold.
  • Forcing the Math: Adjusting the percentage to 80% just because you "really want" the deal. Don't do it.
