Which test should you take?

I genuinely believe that you should take the test that plays to your strengths. The GMAT is often favored for its "Data Insights" and quantitative focus, while the GRE is seen as more accessible for those with strong verbal or vocabulary skills. Fortunately, nearly all top MBA programs now accept both tests equally.

The Conversion Methodology: This tool uses the standard formula provided by ETS (the creators of the GRE). The formula maps your Verbal and Quantitative scores to a predicted GMAT score between 200 and 800.

GMAT Score Breakdown:

  • 700+: Elite. Competitive for Harvard, Stanford, and Wharton.
  • 650 - 690: Strong. Competitive for most top 25 business schools.
  • 600 - 640: Average. Solid for regional and part-time MBA programs.
