Why 2026 is Different

I genuinely believe that 2026 will be the most significant year for taxpayers in a decade. Most of the individual provisions of the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) are scheduled to expire on December 31, 2025. This means that without new legislation, we will revert to higher tax brackets and lower standard deductions.

What to Expect:

  • Higher Rates: The 12% bracket may return to 15%, and the top rate may climb from 37% back to 39.6%.
  • Lower Standard Deduction: The standard deduction is expected to drop significantly (roughly halved, then adjusted for inflation).
  • Personal Exemptions: These may return, potentially benefiting large families.
  • SALT Cap: The $10,000 limit on State and Local Tax deductions may expire, benefiting residents of high-tax states.

Disclaimer: These are projections based on current sunset provisions. Tax laws are subject to change by Congress. This tool provides an estimate for planning purposes only.
