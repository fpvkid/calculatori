Which shutters should you choose?

I genuinely believe that waiting until a storm is named to buy shutters is the worst financial mistake a homeowner can make. Prices double, and contractors disappear. If you live in a hurricane zone, shutters aren't an "option"—they are insurance for your roof (if windows break, air pressure can lift your roof right off).

Accordion vs Roll-down: Accordion shutters are the "sweet spot." They are permanently attached, easy for one person to close in minutes, and cost half of what automatic roll-downs do. If you have a multi-story house, roll-downs are better because you can close them with a remote from inside.

Installation Tips:

  • Permits: Most Florida counties require a permit for permanent shutters.
  • Insurance Discounts: Many insurance companies give a discount if your whole house is "hardened" with shutters.
  • DIY: Storm panels can be DIY-ed, but they are heavy, sharp, and a pain to store. Be careful!
