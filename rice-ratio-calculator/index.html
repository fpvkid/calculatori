The Science of Fluffy Rice

I genuinely believe that the "2-to-1 ratio" is a myth that leads to mushy rice. Most modern rice varieties require less water than people think. The goal is to provide enough liquid for the rice to absorb and just enough extra to account for evaporation during the cooking process.

The "Rinse" Rule: You should always rinse your rice in cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess surface starch, which is the primary culprit behind sticky, gummy rice.

Variety Guide:

  • Basmati: Needs a 1:1.5 ratio and a 15-minute rest (off heat) after cooking to allow the long grains to firm up.
  • Jasmine: A delicate floral rice that thrives with slightly less water (1:1.25) to maintain its structure.
  • Brown Rice: Because the bran layer is intact, it requires significantly more water (1:2) and a longer cook time (45 minutes).
  • Sushi Rice: Requires a low ratio (1:1.1) and a quick soak before cooking to ensure the grains are sticky but still distinct.
