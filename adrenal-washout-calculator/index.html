Interpretation Guidelines

I genuinely believe that diagnostic accuracy saves lives. When an adrenal mass is discovered, radiologists use "washout" calculations to determine if the mass is likely a benign adenoma or a more suspicious lesion.

Standard Criteria:

  • Absolute Washout > 60%: Highly suggestive of a benign adrenal adenoma.
  • Relative Washout > 40%: Suggestive of a benign adrenal adenoma.
  • Non-contrast HU < 10: Usually diagnostic of a lipid-rich adenoma (no washout needed).
