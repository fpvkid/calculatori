Pressure vs. Patience

I genuinely believe that the choice between a slow cooker and an Instant Pot is about lifestyle, not just physics. The slow cooker is perfect for "set it and forget it" morning routines, while the Instant Pot is a life-saver for dinner inspiration that hits at 5:30 PM.

The Golden Rule: Pressure cooking isn't just about heat; it's about raising the boiling point of water. This allows food to cook at much higher temperatures than a standard pot or slow cooker, breaking down tough proteins in a fraction of the time.

Conversion Secrets:

  • Liquid: You need less liquid in an Instant Pot because there is almost no evaporation. For stews, you can often reduce water/broth by 25-50%.
  • Meat: Tough cuts like chuck roast or pork shoulder take about 20-30 minutes per pound under high pressure.
  • Release: Remember to factor in the "Natural Release" time (usually 10-15 minutes) for meats to prevent them from becoming tough due to a sudden pressure drop.
