The Art of Fantasy Naming

I genuinely believe that a name is the foundation of a character. It dictates how people react to them and sets the tone for their story. A sharp, guttural name like "Korgar" immediately suggests a different personality than a soft, melodic name like "Elowen."

Linguistic Logic: Our generator isn't just picking random letters. It uses phonetic rules for each race. Elven names favor vowels and soft consonants (L, S, N), while Dwarven names prioritize hard consonants (D, G, K, R) to reflect their stoic and earthy nature.

Naming Conventions:

  • Humans: Often a mix of classic sounds and evocative surnames (e.g., Storm, Iron, Woods).
  • Elves: Nature-inspired and flowing. They often lack harsh stops in their speech.
  • Dwarves: Short, punchy, and solid. Surnames often reflect a clan or a craft (e.g., Hammer, Forge).
  • Orcs: Guttural and intimidating. They value strength and simplicity in their language.
